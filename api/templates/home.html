Introduction and Motivation

Our project aims to tackle the significant problem of medication non-adherence. It is a widespread issue that affects many people, especially those with chronic conditions, older adults, and individuals with disabilities. Our solution is a smart medication dispenser that reminds users when it's time to take their medication, thus increasing adherence to the medication regimen. To ensure that our dispenser is accessible to all users, we employed a universal design approach, ensuring that it is usable by individuals with various physical abilities and for any type of medication. We created a 3D printed prototype, which allowed us to visualize the product better, identify design issues and refine the design to ensure that it met our specifications. The prototype also helped us to test the functionality and usability of the dispenser, giving us valuable feedback on how to make it better.

Key Insights from Peer Feedback

Overall, it seems like our presentation was received positively by the audience. We did a good job introducing the problem and establishing our target demographic. The 3D printed model was well done, and many people liked it. However, some people felt like our slides were too wordy and could have been more concise. We also received feedback that we should have included more diverse groups of people in our consideration, such as those with mobility issues or who take medications while out and about. Additionally, we could have improved the presentation by not reading off of the slides as much and being more natural.

Conclusion and Future Work

Our project has a great real-world impact, especially for elderly patients. It is a universal design that could apply to everyone, but we could still make it more accessible for people with mobility issues or who have to take medications while out and about. It is a good idea, but it could be expanded upon in the future to differentiate between medication in the event of having to take multiple medicines at the same time. We received positive feedback on our medication dispenser prototype, and we can continue to work on it in the future to make it more accessible and improve its functionality. Future work could include differentiating between medication in the event of having to take multiple medicines at the same time and making the dispenser more accessible for people with mobility issues or who have to take medications while out and about. We could additionally create a mobile app to alert patients when they are not present.
